## Structure-only rewrite

The company should offer a monthly wellness stipend to all employees, but only if the program is affordable and employees who cannot use typical wellness services are treated fairly. The program is affordable if it costs less than the current employee engagement budget. Fair treatment means every employee receives the same gym reimbursement, whether or not they can use a gym.

Employees report high stress, health insurance does not cover many preventive activities, and [G3: evidence that stress and health insurance gaps contribute to burnout] the company is concerned about burnout.

If employees receive a wellness stipend, [G4: evidence or reasoning that wellness stipends reduce turnover] they will have more options for managing stress, feel more supported by the company, and be less likely to quit.

Standard wellness services means gym memberships, fitness classes, diet programs, and company-approved meditation apps. The policy should not disadvantage employees who live in areas without gyms, employees with disabilities, employees with caregiving responsibilities, religious restrictions, or employees who prefer nontraditional wellness practices. Employees who cannot use gyms will receive no separate benefit because the stipend is meant to encourage healthier habits.
