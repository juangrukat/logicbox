This thesis presents innovative optimization and learning methodologies
aimed at advancing vision-based quality inspection systems.
Specifically, the work is motivated by the need to passively inspect
Body-In-White (BIW) gaps and flush dimensions of vehicles from a
distance during manufacturing. We need low-cost inspection methods to
learn from relatively lesser amounts of ground truth dimensional data
and operate on vehicles moving and rotating in multiple directions. To
operate in this challenging environment, we need to match the measure
areas on the vehicles by solving an unsupervised learning problem.
Central to this research is the development of learning and
optimization framework that enhances the accuracy and efficiency of
visual inspections, even under dynamic conditions and with limited
training data. This dissertation starts by proposing a general
framework, Exploratory Image Data Analysis (EIDA), to teach quality
engineers and practitioners how to work with image data. As an
extension of Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) in quality improvement
problems, EIDA implements image as the input data and the goal is to
extract useful information from the image inputs for exploration of
potential problems and causal effects and further serves as a guiding
tool for optimization and learning. This part of the dissertation
presents a practical framework for EIDA in the quality improvement
projects with four major steps of EIDA: image pre-processing,
image-derived data processing and display, salient feature
identification, and salient feature interpretation. For this purpose,
various case studies are presented alongside the major steps and tried
to discuss these steps with various visualization techniques. Because
of the challenges including relatively tiny amounts of supervised
dimensional data, little (if any) target detection image data, moving
targets, and the need for prediction errors below 0.5 mm, we find
little (if any) directly pertinent image-based research. Our key idea
is to combine Optimal Transport (OT) theory to address our dynamic
rotating and shifting subjects with Markov Decision Process (MDP)
agents, to leverage the patterns of movement in the assembly line, and
with polynomial regression to predict dimensions in the context of
lesser amounts of supervised data. Our approach of OT-based image
matching with Procrustes Analysis emphasizes the precise alignment of
edge points within images, enabling accurate similarity assessments
between different image structures and facilitating the learning of
geometric transformations between image pairs. This research
significantly enhances defect detection accuracy and improves the
reliability of dimensional measurements, which are critical for
high-accuracy inspections. By using an MDP formulation, we enable
Reinforcement Learning (RL) techniques, permitting the inspection
system to autonomously learn optimal tracking strategies through
interaction with dynamic environments. Additionally, by integrating
optimal transport with the RL agent, target detection and tracking
become effective even with limited training data, eliminating the need
for extensive labeling of images required in many supervised learning
formulations. In addition, it achieves an average detection accuracy
above 93% while increasing frames per second by over 100 times compared
with a relevant grid search method. A key contribution of this work is
our combined method framework EIDA, OT-based image matching, RL-driven
tracking, and regression-based modeling to achieve high levels of
accuracy for low-cost camera systems. The integrated framework not only
streamlines the inspection workflow but also offers scalability and
adaptability across diverse manufacturing contexts.
